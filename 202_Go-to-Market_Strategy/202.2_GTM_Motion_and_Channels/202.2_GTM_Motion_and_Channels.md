## Purpose
Translates the strategic positioning from `202.1_GTM_Foundation/202.1_GTM_Strategy.md` into a concrete, channel-specific execution system. This document defines the sales motion design, channel prioritization logic, and ICP-to-channel routing rules.

**Effective this version**, all motion design is anchored on the **Tier 1 ICP: The Seed Founder ("Zero-to-Sales")**. All channel, messaging, and offer decisions default to serving this persona first. Tier 2 ICPs are reached via ecosystem/partner channels and inbound — not via primary outbound resources.

---

## 102. The [CLIENT_NAME] Sales Motion (Founder-First Design)

The core sales motion is tuned for a founder who has product-market fit but no sales team, no repeatble playbook, and no sales infrastructure.

### Step 1 — The "First Sales Hire" Intercept
- **Trigger**: The founder is about to make a costly first SDR hire (or has just posted the job).
- **[CLIENT_NAME] Message**: "Before you spend $8k–$12k/mo on a junior SDR you'll have to manage, let us run a 14-day Sprint. If we don't book you a qualified meeting, you owe nothing. If we do, you've just proved your outbound model — and we become your engine."
- **Outcome**: Converts the "about to hire" intent into a Sprint commitment.

### Step 2 — The Manual Waste Audit (Adapted for Founders)
- We map out the founder's current sales activities (personal network, LinkedIn DMs, warm intros).
- We produce a **"Founder Time Audit"** showing how many hours/week the founder spends on sales activities vs. what it would cost to delegate those to [CLIENT_NAME]'s infrastructure.
- Outcome: Creates urgency and quantifies the cost of doing nothing.

### Step 3 — The Blueprint Session
- We co-design the GTM architecture with the founder. They see their future system before signing.
- Deliverable: A visual GTM Architecture Map — their "Autonomous GTM Spine."
- Outcome: Founder is emotionally invested. This is *their* system, not a vendor's platform.

### Step 4 — The 14-Day Sprint
- A low-risk, time-boxed pilot. [CLIENT_NAME] deploys the full stack for one ICP segment.
- Deliverable: At least 1 qualified, meeting-booked lead within 14 days of activation.
- Outcome: Converts from Sprint to GTM Engine retainer.

## 201. Channel Architecture & Prioritization

### 🥇 Priority 1 (Tier 1 ICP Focus): Direct Outbound to Founders
*[CLIENT_NAME]'s own agents target Seed Founders directly. This is the primary lead generation engine.*

- **Email Outreach (Autonomous)**
    - **Target**: Founder/Co-Founder/CEO at Seed-stage B2B SaaS companies (1–15 employees).
    - **Qualifying Signal Used**: Recent funding round announcement, job post for "first sales hire," Product Hunt launch, or tech stack detection (HubSpot Free / no CRM).
    - **Messaging Hook**: Lead with the "First Sales Hire Intercept" angle. Reference the specific signal found by the agent.
    - **Signature line**: "This message was researched and sent by a [CLIENT_NAME] agent — the same infrastructure I'm offering you."

- **LinkedIn Outreach (Autonomous)**
    - **Target**: Founders actively posting about founder-led sales challenges, GTM struggles, or hiring for sales.
    - **Approach**: Comment strategically on posts before connecting. DM sequences focus on the "hiring vs. building" decision.
    - **Content parallel**: Authority-building posts aimed at the founder audience (pain-first content, not feature-first).

### 🥈 Priority 2: Outbound to Tier 2 ICPs
*Secondary outbound for Series A-C companies. Lower volume; higher ACV.*

- **Email Outreach**: Target VP Sales and Head of RevOps at Series A–B companies.
- **LinkedIn**: Engage RevOps communities and sales leader groups.
- **Trigger**: Only run Tier 2 sequences when Tier 1 pipeline is healthy (>10 active prospects).

### 🥉 Priority 3: Ecosystem / Partnership Channels (Tier 2 ICP Path)
*Inbound from partner networks. Lower CAC; higher trust.*

- **VC/PE Portfolio Program**
    - Target partners: Seed and Series A VCs with active portfolios.
    - Value proposition to VCs: "Offer [CLIENT_NAME] as 'GTM Infrastructure' to your portfolio's Seed founders — helps them de-risk the outbound motion before their first sales hire."
    - Entry point: Warm intro to portfolio founders at exactly the right stage.

- **CRM Implementation Partners**
    - Target: HubSpot consultancies onboarding startups with empty CRMs.
    - Value proposition: "After you set up the CRM, we fill it — autonomously."

### 🎙️ Priority 4: Content & Authority (Inbound Warm-Up)
*Warms the Founder audience and reduces outbound friction.*

- **"Build in Public" Content Motion**
    - Format: LinkedIn posts written *for founders*, sharing "Agent Logs" — anonymized examples of how [CLIENT_NAME] identified a prospect signal and booked a meeting autonomously.
    - Frequency: 3x per week minimum.
    - Tone: Direct, founder-to-founder. Not corporate. Transparent about the AI.

- **"First Sales Hire vs. [CLIENT_NAME]" Comparison Content**
    - Format: Written breakdowns: "The Real Cost of Your First SDR vs. [CLIENT_NAME]'s 14-Day Sprint."
    - Goal: Capture the "I'm about to make my first sales hire" search and intent.

- **Architecture Sessions (Founder-focused Webinars)**
    - Format: Live or recorded sessions where [CLIENT_NAME] maps out a GTM spine for a founder volunteer.
    - Goal: Social proof, content library, and warm pipeline from founders watching.

## 203. Founder Messaging Framework

All outbound messaging to Tier 1 Founders must follow this framework:

102. **Signal Reference**: Open with the specific signal that triggered the outreach. ("I saw you just closed your seed round" / "I noticed you posted about founder-led sales" / "I saw you're hiring your first SDR.")
201. **Pain Mirroring**: Acknowledge the exact situation they're in. ("Most founders in your position are spending 10–15 hours/week on sales but haven't built a repeatable motion yet.")
202. **Reframe**: Introduce the alternative. ("Before making that first SDR hire, there's a faster and cheaper way to prove the motion.")
203. **The Offer**: The 14-Day Sprint. One sentence. ("We run a 14-day autonomous outbound Sprint — if we don't book you a qualified meeting, you don't pay.")
301. **CTA**: A single, low-friction ask. ("Worth a 15-minute call to see if the timing is right?")

---

## 301. Channel Rules & Constraints

- **Signal-anchored messaging is non-negotiable**: Every outbound touch must reference at least one specific, researched signal. Generic blasts are prohibited.
- **Founder-first resource allocation**: Primary outbound agent capacity (email volume, LinkedIn activity) is reserved for Tier 1 ICP. Tier 2 runs on secondary capacity.
- **CRM hygiene**: All activity syncs to CRM in real-time. No orphaned contacts. For Tier 1 Seed Founders, [CLIENT_NAME] may *also* provision their CRM as part of the Sprint.
- **Content-outbound coordination**: Target founders on the outbound list should also see content being served to them on LinkedIn before sequence contact (parallel warm).
