## 2. Objection Handling Library

### Core Tone Profiles
- **Authoritative:** Direct, fact-based. Use when prospect is logical or comparing tools.
- **Empathetic:** Warmer, acknowledges pain. Use when prospect is frustrated by past failures.
- **Urgent:** Time-to-value focused. Use when prospect is busy or dismissive.

### Category 1: "Is this just AI hype or another wrapper?"
- **Variation A — Authoritative:** *"No. The core problem we solve is not model access, it's the lack of a machine-readable company context layer. Most agent projects fail because the company is unreadable to the agent, not because the model is weak."*
- **Variation B — Empathetic:** *"That skepticism makes sense. A lot of teams have already tried prompts, copilots, or automations and still ended up with brittle results. We start one layer deeper by structuring the company context those tools depend on."*
- **Variation C — Urgent:** *"If this were just another wrapper, it would not be worth your time. The practical question is whether your team is losing time because agents and automations cannot trust your operational context. If yes, this is infrastructure, not hype."*

### Category 2: Pricing / Budget Objections
- **"It's too expensive."**
  - Angle: Reframe from software line item to operational risk and repeated context reconstruction cost
  - Variation A — Authoritative: *"The real comparison is not against a cheaper tool. It is against the cost of your team repeatedly reconstructing context, checking approvals manually, and delaying agent adoption because the workflow is not grounded."*
  - Variation B — Empathetic: *"Understood. Early infrastructure work can feel hard to justify until the pain is visible. That is why we keep the first engagement narrow: one workflow, one sprint, one concrete blueprint instead of a broad platform commitment."*

- **"We don't have budget right now."**
  - Angle: Redirect to a smaller entry offer
  - Response: *"That usually means the problem is real but not yet prioritized. In that case, the best next step is not a large rollout. It is a scoped conversation around one workflow so you can see whether the cost of waiting is actually higher than the cost of solving it."*

### Category 3: Trust / Risk Objections
- **"We've been burned by agencies before."**
  - Angle: Differentiate infrastructure design from black-box delivery
  - Variation A — Authoritative: *"That concern is valid. Our approach is deliberately transparent: we show the logic, the workflow map, the evidence layer, and the access model. You are not buying hidden execution, you are buying a grounded operating blueprint your team can inspect."*
  - Variation B — Empathetic: *"That makes sense. A lot of teams have paid for outputs without gaining a reusable internal system. We focus on building a memory and workflow foundation you can keep using after the engagement ends."*

- **"How do I know this will work for us?"**
  - Angle: Use the sprint offer as proof mechanism
  - Response: *"You do not need to believe in a full platform rollout upfront. The 21-day sprint exists to answer that question with one real workflow. If we cannot make that workflow clearer, more grounded, and safer for agent interaction, you should not expand further."*

### Category 4: Competitor / Alternative Objections
- **"We're looking at another AI platform."**
  - Angle: Separate tool capability from company readiness
  - Response: *"That may still be useful, but it solves a different layer. Most platforms assume the company context already exists in a structured, reliable form. PulseOS-KB focuses on the layer those tools depend on: the company memory, workflow logic, and evidence grounding that determine whether any agent system can work reliably."*

- **"We already use an AI tool stack."**
  - Angle: Complement, not replace
  - Response: *"That is usually a good sign, not a blocker. PulseOS-KB does not require you to replace the stack. We help make the underlying company context usable so the tools you already have can operate with more reliability and less manual checking."*

### Category 5: Timing Objections
- **"We're not ready yet."**
  - Response: *"That may be true for a full rollout, but the sprint is designed for companies that are not ready for broad deployment. It helps you get ready by defining one workflow clearly enough to evaluate safely."*

- **"Let's revisit next quarter."**
  - Response: *"That is reasonable if the issue is not operationally painful yet. Before we push this out, the useful question is whether the workflow friction you described will actually be smaller next quarter or just more expensive because the team keeps working around it manually."*

## 3. AI Handover & Escalation Protocols

### Handover Triggers
| Trigger | Action | Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Prospect explicitly requests a human | Transfer immediately | 🔴 Critical |
| Pricing negotiation beyond approved range | Escalate to sales rep | 🔴 Critical |
| Legal, compliance, or contract questions | Escalate to sales rep + legal | 🔴 Critical |
| Prospect expresses strong frustration | De-escalate, then transfer | 🔴 Critical |
| Enterprise or multi-workflow expansion request | Escalate to founder-led sales review before scoping | 🟠 High |
| Technical question beyond script | Flag and log for follow-up | 🟡 Medium |
| Conversation exceeds 3 objection cycles | Transfer — human closes better | 🟡 Medium |

### Handover Protocol (Step-by-Step)
**Step 1 — Signal the handover:**
> *"I want to make sure you get the most accurate answer. Let me connect you with a PulseOS-KB human rep who can help with your specific pricing, legal, or implementation question."*

**Step 2 — Log full context to CRM:**
- Prospect name, company, contact info
- Summary of conversation (key pain points surfaced)
- Objections raised and handling status
- Qualification status: Qualified / Partially Qualified / Unknown
- Trigger for handover

**Step 3 — Notify the human rep:**
- Notification method: As an inference from the available intake materials, Slack `#sales-alerts` plus CRM task creation
- SLA for human follow-up: Within 2 business hours on business days

**Step 4 — Set prospect expectation:**
> *"You'll hear from a PulseOS-KB team member within 2 business hours. I'm passing along a summary of what we discussed so you do not need to repeat yourself."*

### CRM Tag Taxonomy
| Tag | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- |
| `[AI_QUALIFIED]` | All BANT criteria met. High-priority follow-up. |
| `[AI_PARTIALLY_QUALIFIED]` | Need confirmed, budget/authority unclear. |
| `[AI_DISQUALIFIED]` | Does not meet ICP criteria. Archive. |
| `[PRICING_ESCALATION]` | Requested pricing outside approved range. |

| `[EXISTING_CLIENT]` | Route to account management, not sales. |

### Do-Not-Contact Rules
The AI agent must never contact:
- Anyone who has previously unsubscribed or opted out
- Contacts tagged `[DO_NOT_CONTACT]` in CRM
- Existing clients (route to account management)
- Prospects marked lost with reason `[NOT_A_FIT]`

## 6. Data Privacy & Security (Enterprise Reference)

### Legal Basis for Processing
PulseOS-KB operates under **Legitimate Interest (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR)** for B2B outreach. As an inference from the available intake materials, this applies to narrowly targeted business outreach tied to relevant operational pain points rather than mass-market consumer marketing.

### Data Minimization & Retention
- **Collection:** Minimum PII required: full name, work email, company name, role/title, business context notes, and outreach status
- **Retention:** Data retained only for the duration of the engagement; purged or transferred per DPA or contract terms on termination
- **Opt-Out:** Clear unsubscribe or direct opt-out handling in every outbound sequence, with immediate CRM suppression on request

### Infrastructure Security
- **Domain Isolation:** As an inference from the available intake materials, outreach and workflow access should be isolated by environment and scoped to the minimum systems necessary for the sprint
- **Protocol Enforcement:** SPF, DKIM, DMARC on all outreach domains
- **Integration Security:** OAuth where available; otherwise scoped API keys with least-privilege access
- **AI Non-Training Clause:** As an inference from the available intake materials, client data used in PulseOS-KB engagements should not be used to train public models unless explicitly approved in writing by the client
