# Migma GTM Multi-Channel Qualified Lead Campaign Playbook

## Purpose
Document the minimum viable multi-channel campaign system for targeting companies that use Klaviyo, Mailchimp, or SendGrid, running Migma-powered email outreach, LinkedIn outreach, PPC campaigns, and website visitor tracking through one qualified-lead feedback loop.

## Executive Summary
- The campaign should not try to target exact Apollo contacts directly inside ad platforms unless the data is first-party and legally usable for ad targeting.
- The practical motion is account-first:
  - identify companies using Klaviyo, Mailchimp, or SendGrid
  - enrich specific buyer contacts
  - run coordinated email, LinkedIn, PPC, and website tracking
  - qualify inbound leads through a form
  - send qualified-lead and intent events back into the CRM, Migma, Meta, Google, and retargeting audiences
- Start with one platform segment, preferably Klaviyo, before testing Mailchimp and SendGrid.
- Minimum useful one-month media budget is approximately `$3,000`.
- Bare-minimum signal budget is `$1,500-$2,000`, but this is likely to validate messaging only, not produce reliable optimization data.

## Core Strategy
### Target Account Definition
- Target companies that use one of:
  - Klaviyo
  - Mailchimp
  - SendGrid
  - Twilio SendGrid Email API
  - Mailchimp Mandrill
  - Mailchimp Transactional Email
- Prioritize companies with:
  - ecommerce, SaaS, marketplace, or subscription revenue
  - active email volume
  - visible lifecycle, growth, or marketing operations complexity
  - urgent pain around deliverability, automations, segmentation, revenue leakage, or migration

### Target Person Definition
- Primary buyers:
  - Founder
  - CEO
  - Head of Growth
  - Head of Marketing
  - Lifecycle Marketing Lead
  - Email Marketing Manager
  - Marketing Operations Lead
  - RevOps Lead
- Secondary validators:
  - Ecommerce Manager
  - CRM Manager
  - Technical Marketing Manager
  - Product Marketing Lead

### Channel Role
- Migma email campaigns:
  - Primary direct-response outbound motion.
  - Use company-level technology signal and contact-level persona enrichment.
  - Personalize by platform, pain, role, and observed website behavior.
- LinkedIn outreach:
  - Warm parallel touchpoint to the same specific leads.
  - Use connection requests, profile views, comments, and short follow-ups to increase familiarity before or after email.
- Google Search:
  - Capture active pain and buying intent.
  - Best for high-intent keywords around platform problems.
- Meta:
  - Test creative hooks cheaply.
  - Retarget visitors and form starters.
  - Let creative self-qualify the audience.
- Google YouTube / Demand Gen / Display:
  - Build awareness around platform-specific problems.
  - Use custom segments from platform keywords and URLs.
- LinkedIn:
  - Best channel for exact account and role targeting if budget allows.
  - Use after the company list exists.
- Website visitor tracking:
  - Identify account-level intent from visitors who do not submit forms.
  - Trigger Migma email and LinkedIn follow-up when target accounts visit key pages.

## Data Sources And Audience Building
### Apollo
- Apollo can filter people by employer technologies using:
  - `currently_using_any_of_technology_uids[]`
  - `currently_using_all_of_technology_uids[]`
  - `currently_not_using_any_of_technology_uids[]`
- Example People API Search body:

```json
{
  "currently_using_any_of_technology_uids": [
    "klaviyo",
    "mailchimp",
    "sendgrid"
  ],
  "person_titles": [
    "Head of Marketing",
    "Lifecycle Marketing Manager",
    "Email Marketing Manager",
    "Growth Manager"
  ],
  "per_page": 100
}
```

- Apollo's technology signal is employer-level.
- It does not prove the person personally uses the platform.
- Use Apollo mainly for contact enrichment after a target account list exists.

### BuiltWith
- Best for sourcing website-level technology lists.
- Useful for:
  - Klaviyo users
  - Shopify plus Klaviyo users
  - Mailchimp users
  - SendGrid users
- Use the Lists API to export domains by technology.
- Use `OTHERTECHS` when combining filters, such as Shopify plus Klaviyo.

### Wappalyzer
- Best for validating or enriching a known domain list.
- Use for:
  - bulk domain technology lookup
  - cached or live technology validation
  - checking if a company still appears to use the platform

### HG Insights
- Better for deeper enterprise technographics.
- Use only if:
  - enterprise accounts matter
  - backend or behind-the-firewall technology detection matters
  - budget supports paid technographic data

### Recommended Data Flow
1. Source companies from BuiltWith or Wappalyzer.
2. Validate and segment the company list.
3. Use Apollo to enrich likely buyers at those companies.
4. Load the account and contact list into Migma for email sequencing.
5. Use the same contacts for manual or semi-automated LinkedIn outreach.
6. Use the company list and persona insights to guide PPC creative and landing pages.
7. Track website visitors and join account-level web intent back to the outreach list.
8. Do not upload Apollo-sourced contact lists directly into Google Customer Match.

## Compliance Guardrails
### Google
- Customer Match is for first-party data collected directly by the advertiser.
- Do not upload Apollo or third-party prospect emails unless there is a lawful first-party basis and the data complies with Google policy.
- Use Google Search, custom segments, and offline conversion import instead of direct third-party list targeting.

### Meta
- Customer list custom audiences require rights, permissions, lawful basis, and opt-out compliance.
- Meta may accept hashed customer data, but the data still needs to be legally usable.
- For this campaign, prefer:
  - website traffic audiences
  - lead form submitters
  - qualified first-party leads
  - lookalikes from first-party qualified leads

### Email And LinkedIn
- Follow applicable cold outreach rules for the target geography.
- Use business-relevant messaging, a clear sender identity, and simple opt-out.
- Do not imply individual platform usage unless the signal is actually person-level.
- Safer language:
  - "Your company appears to use Klaviyo."
  - "We help teams running lifecycle campaigns on Klaviyo."
- Riskier language:
  - "I saw you personally use Klaviyo."
  - "You are leaking revenue in your account."

## Campaign Architecture
### Multi-Channel Account Motion
- One target account should move through all channels as one record.
- The same account can receive:
  - Migma email sequence
  - LinkedIn profile view or connection request
  - PPC impression or retargeting impression
  - website tracking event
  - form submission and qualification
- The CRM or campaign database should store:
  - account domain
  - platform signal
  - contact identity
  - channel touches
  - website visits
  - form submissions
  - qualification status
  - booked call status
  - customer status
- The operating metric is cost and effort per qualified opportunity, not channel-isolated CPL.

### Initial Segment
- Start with Klaviyo only.
- Reason:
  - clearer ecommerce/lifecycle use case
  - strong platform-specific pain
  - better creative hooks
  - easier landing page specificity

### Offer
- Primary offer:
  - Free Klaviyo Flow + Revenue Leak Audit
- Alternate offer angles:
  - Klaviyo deliverability audit
  - abandoned revenue audit
  - lifecycle automation teardown
  - peak-season email readiness audit

### Migma Email Campaign
- Campaign name:
  - `MigmaGTM - Klaviyo - Email - Revenue Leak Audit`
- Audience:
  - companies using Klaviyo
  - buyer contacts enriched from Apollo
  - target roles in growth, lifecycle, email, marketing ops, RevOps, founder, or CEO
- Sequence:
  - email 1:
    - direct platform-specific audit offer
  - email 2:
    - pain-specific follow-up based on role
  - email 3:
    - short teardown/checklist angle
  - email 4:
    - final useful asset or audit invitation
- Personalization fields:
  - `company_name`
  - `platform`
  - `role_category`
  - `industry`
  - `estimated_email_volume`
  - `detected_stack`
  - `website_visit_signal`
- Core CTA:
  - route to the same landing page and qualification form used by PPC.
- Tracking:
  - append UTMs to every email link.
  - store email opens and clicks as channel touches.
  - use clicks and website visits to prioritize LinkedIn and manual follow-up.

### LinkedIn Outreach
- Campaign name:
  - `MigmaGTM - Klaviyo - LinkedIn - Warm Parallel Touch`
- Audience:
  - same contacts used in Migma email campaigns.
- Touch pattern:
  - view profile before or after email 1.
  - connect with a short non-pitch message.
  - after acceptance, send a concise platform-specific audit note.
  - engage with relevant posts only when natural and useful.
- Message principle:
  - do not duplicate the email body.
  - LinkedIn should create familiarity and route warm leads to the audit page or a direct conversation.
- Example connection note:
  - "Saw your team is in ecommerce growth. I am researching Klaviyo revenue leaks across lifecycle teams and thought it would be useful to connect."
- Example follow-up:
  - "We are offering a short Klaviyo flow and revenue leak audit for teams with active lifecycle programs. Worth sending over the checklist?"

### Google Search Campaign
- Campaign name:
  - `MigmaGTM - Klaviyo - Search - Audit`
- Keyword themes:
  - `klaviyo agency`
  - `klaviyo consultant`
  - `klaviyo audit`
  - `klaviyo flow setup`
  - `klaviyo email automation`
  - `klaviyo deliverability`
  - `klaviyo abandoned cart flow`
  - `klaviyo revenue`
- Match type:
  - Start with exact and phrase match.
  - Add broad match only after conversion quality is proven.
- Negative keyword themes:
  - jobs
  - salary
  - login
  - pricing only
  - free templates
  - certification
  - course
  - tutorial

### Meta Prospecting Campaign
- Campaign objective:
  - Leads or website conversions.
- Initial optimization event:
  - `Lead`
- Audience:
  - Broad ecommerce/growth audience
  - Platform interest signals if available
  - Advantage+ audience with audience suggestions
- Creative should self-qualify:
  - "For Klaviyo teams sending 50k+ emails/month"
  - "Find the flows leaking revenue inside your Klaviyo account"
  - "Still relying on generic flows while your list keeps growing?"
  - "Free Klaviyo revenue leak audit for ecommerce operators"

### Retargeting Campaign
- Retarget:
  - landing page visitors
  - form starters
  - video viewers
  - lead magnet visitors
  - pricing or audit page visitors
- Exclude:
  - submitted leads
  - booked calls
  - customers
- Creative:
  - proof
  - case-style breakdowns
  - audit screenshots or anonymized examples
  - "See what we check in a Klaviyo audit"

### Google YouTube / Demand Gen / Display
- Use custom segments with:
  - keywords:
    - `Klaviyo flows`
    - `Klaviyo deliverability`
    - `Klaviyo abandoned cart`
    - `email marketing automation`
    - `Shopify email marketing`
  - URLs:
    - `klaviyo.com`
    - `help.klaviyo.com`
    - competitor and agency pages
- Important:
  - Google custom segments do not target exact visitors to those URLs.
  - They target people with similar interests, search behavior, or purchase intent.

### B2B Website Visitor Tracking
- Goal:
  - identify which target accounts visit the landing page or website before they submit a form.
- Tool options:
  - RB2B
  - Warmly
  - Leadfeeder
  - Clearbit Reveal
  - 6sense
  - Factors.ai
  - Snitcher
- Capture:
  - company domain
  - page visited
  - session timestamp
  - source and campaign
  - repeat visit count
  - form start or submit status
  - matched target account status
- Trigger rules:
  - if a target account visits the Klaviyo audit page, prioritize that account in Migma.
  - if a known contact clicks email and visits the page, create a high-priority follow-up task.
  - if an account has multiple visits and no form submission, add to retargeting and LinkedIn follow-up.
  - if an account visits pricing, audit, or case-study pages, mark as high intent.

## Lead Scoring
### Statuses
- `raw_lead`
- `qualified_lead`
- `sales_accepted_lead`
- `booked_call`
- `customer`
- `disqualified`

### Basic Qualification Rule
- Mark as `qualified_lead` if:
  - platform is Klaviyo, Mailchimp, or SendGrid
  - company type is ecommerce, SaaS, marketplace, or subscription business
  - monthly email volume is above `10k`
  - role is founder, growth, marketing, lifecycle, RevOps, or marketing ops
  - pain is deliverability, automation, revenue leakage, segmentation, migration, or reporting
  - work email is present

### Suggested Score Model
- Platform fit:
  - Klaviyo: `+25`
  - Mailchimp: `+15`
  - SendGrid: `+15`
  - Other: `0`
- Company type:
  - Ecommerce: `+25`
  - SaaS: `+15`
  - Marketplace: `+15`
  - Agency: `+5`
- Monthly email volume:
  - 250k+: `+25`
  - 50k-250k: `+20`
  - 10k-50k: `+10`
  - under 10k: `0`
- Role:
  - Founder / CEO: `+20`
  - Growth / Marketing leader: `+20`
  - Lifecycle / Email marketer: `+15`
  - RevOps / Marketing Ops: `+15`
  - Other: `0`
- Urgent pain:
  - Deliverability: `+20`
  - Revenue leakage: `+20`
  - Automation setup: `+15`
  - Migration: `+15`
  - Segmentation: `+10`
  - Reporting: `+10`

### Fit Tiers
- `A` fit:
  - score `80+`
  - immediate follow-up
- `B` fit:
  - score `60-79`
  - nurture and manual review
- `C` fit:
  - score `40-59`
  - retarget and nurture
- `D` fit:
  - score below `40`
  - disqualify or low-priority nurture

## Tracking And Conversion Events
### Unified Account Timeline
- Every account should have one timeline across:
  - Migma email sends, opens, clicks, replies, bounces, and opt-outs
  - LinkedIn profile views, connection requests, accepts, messages, and replies
  - PPC campaign, ad group, ad, click, and conversion touchpoints
  - website visits, page depth, repeat visits, and form starts
  - form submissions, qualification score, booked calls, and revenue
- Required identifiers:
  - `account_domain`
  - `company_name`
  - `contact_email`
  - `linkedin_url`
  - `lead_id`
  - `campaign_id`
  - `utm_campaign`
  - `utm_source`
  - `utm_medium`
- The system should answer:
  - which accounts were touched
  - which contacts engaged
  - which channel created the first touch
  - which channel created the converting touch
  - which account became qualified
  - which qualified accounts booked calls

### Browser Events
- `PageView`
- `ViewContent`
- `LeadFormStart`
- `Lead`
- `AccountVisit`
- `HighIntentVisit`
- `EmailClickVisit`

### Server / CRM Events
- `QualifiedLead`
- `SalesAcceptedLead`
- `BookedCall`
- `Customer`
- `EmailReply`
- `LinkedInReply`
- `TargetAccountVisited`
- `RetargetingEligible`

### Event Rules
- Fire `Lead` when the form is submitted.
- Fire `QualifiedLead` only after scoring confirms fit.
- Fire `BookedCall` only when a calendar meeting is booked.
- Fire `Customer` only after a paid conversion.
- Fire `TargetAccountVisited` when a visitor identification tool matches a target account.
- Fire `HighIntentVisit` when a target account visits a high-intent page.
- Fire `EmailReply` or `LinkedInReply` when a specific lead replies.
- Use one stable `lead_id` across all systems.
- Use one stable `account_id` or `account_domain` across account-level systems.
- Use `event_id` for Meta Pixel and Conversions API deduplication.

### Google Ads Setup
- Install Google tag or Google Tag Manager.
- Enable Enhanced Conversions for Leads.
- Capture and store:
  - email
  - phone when available
  - `gclid`
  - `gbraid`
  - `wbraid`
  - lead status
  - conversion timestamp
- Import offline conversions for:
  - `Qualified lead`
  - `Converted lead`
- Start optimization on raw lead volume.
- Move bidding toward qualified lead events once there is enough volume.

### Meta Setup
- Install Meta Pixel.
- Configure Conversions API from backend, CRM, or automation layer.
- Send browser and server events with matching `event_id` for deduplication.
- Send hashed first-party user data where lawful:
  - email
  - phone
  - first name
  - last name
  - city
  - state
  - country
  - zip
- Use CAPI for:
  - `Lead`
  - `QualifiedLead`
  - `BookedCall`
  - `Customer`
- Do not rely only on browser pixel events.

### Migma Setup
- Store every prospect with:
  - account domain
  - platform
  - persona
  - source
  - campaign
  - LinkedIn URL
  - qualification status
- Send email link clicks to the same landing page with UTMs.
- Create follow-up states:
  - `not_contacted`
  - `email_sent`
  - `email_clicked`
  - `email_replied`
  - `linkedin_connected`
  - `site_visited`
  - `form_submitted`
  - `qualified`
  - `booked`
- Suppress:
  - bounced emails
  - opted-out contacts
  - booked calls
  - customers
  - disqualified leads

### LinkedIn Setup
- Store LinkedIn outreach as structured touchpoints.
- Minimum fields:
  - `linkedin_profile_url`
  - `connection_requested_at`
  - `connection_accepted_at`
  - `linkedin_message_sent_at`
  - `linkedin_reply_at`
  - `linkedin_outcome`
- Use LinkedIn replies as a manual sales signal, not an ad-platform conversion unless the reply leads to a tracked form, call booking, or CRM status change.

### Website Visitor Tracking Setup
- Install the visitor identification script on:
  - landing page
  - audit page
  - case-study page
  - pricing or consultation page
- Match visits to:
  - target account list
  - email click records
  - CRM account domain
- Create alerts for:
  - target account visited
  - repeated visit from same account
  - high-intent page visit
  - known email click plus website visit
- Route alerts to:
  - Migma sequence priority
  - CRM task
  - LinkedIn follow-up queue
  - retargeting audience where compliant

## Budget
### Practical One-Month Test
- Minimum useful media budget:
  - `$2,500-$3,500`
- Recommended all-in budget:
  - `$3,000-$4,000`
- Reason:
  - enough traffic to test creative and form conversion
  - enough raw leads to inspect quality
  - possible but not guaranteed qualified-lead learning

### Recommended Budget Split
- Google Search:
  - `$1,500`
- Meta prospecting:
  - `$900`
- Meta retargeting:
  - `$300`
- Reserve / testing buffer:
  - `$300`
- Total media:
  - `$3,000`

### Bare-Minimum Test
- Media budget:
  - `$1,500-$2,000`
- Scope:
  - one segment only
  - one landing page
  - one offer
  - Google Search plus Meta retargeting
- Expected outcome:
  - messaging signal
  - early CPL estimate
  - limited qualified-lead confidence

### Expected Volume Targets
- Landing page visits:
  - `300-600`
- Raw form starts or submits:
  - `20-40`
- Qualified leads:
  - `5-10`
- Booked calls:
  - `1-3`

### Decision Rules
- Good:
  - qualified lead cost below `$150`
  - at least `5` qualified leads
  - at least `1` booked call
- Promising:
  - qualified lead cost between `$150-$300`
  - low volume but strong role and company fit
  - landing page or form has clear fixable drop-off
- Bad:
  - cheap raw leads but no platform fit
  - wrong roles
  - no booked calls
  - high form completion from low-fit companies

## 30-Day Test Plan
### Week 1: Setup
- Build the Klaviyo landing page.
- Build the qualification form.
- Configure hidden attribution fields.
- Create lead scoring logic.
- Build the target account list from technographic sources.
- Enrich contacts through Apollo.
- Load the target contacts into Migma.
- Prepare the LinkedIn outreach list.
- Install website visitor tracking.
- Install Google Tag Manager, Google tag, Meta Pixel, and CAPI.
- Create Google Search campaign.
- Create Meta prospecting and retargeting campaigns.
- QA all events before spend begins.

### Week 2: Launch And Baseline
- Launch the first Migma email sequence.
- Start LinkedIn profile views and connection requests for the same accounts.
- Launch Google Search with exact and phrase match keywords.
- Launch Meta with `3-5` creative angles.
- Monitor:
  - email delivery
  - email reply rate
  - LinkedIn acceptance rate
  - click-through rate
  - landing page conversion rate
  - form start rate
  - form completion rate
  - raw lead quality
  - target account website visits
- Do not over-optimize before conversion data exists.

### Week 3: Quality Review
- Review every lead manually.
- Check:
  - platform fit
  - company type
  - role
  - pain
  - email quality
  - booked-call quality
- Pause creatives that generate low-fit leads.
- Add negatives to Google Search.
- Tighten form copy if low-fit submissions dominate.
- Prioritize follow-up for accounts with:
  - email click
  - LinkedIn acceptance
  - website visit
  - form start without submission
  - repeat visit from target account
- Update Migma sequence logic based on reply quality.

### Week 4: Qualified Signal Push
- Send `QualifiedLead` events back to Meta and Google.
- Compare:
  - cost per raw lead
  - cost per qualified lead
  - cost per booked call
  - email reply to qualified lead rate
  - LinkedIn reply to qualified lead rate
  - website visit to qualified lead rate
  - account engagement to booked call rate
- Decide whether to:
  - continue Klaviyo
  - test Mailchimp
  - test SendGrid
  - rebuild offer or landing page
