## 4. Decision-Making Framework

### Decision Types

| Type | Who Decides | Consultation Required |
|---|---|---|
| Day-to-day operational | Team member / Agent | None |
| Process or tool change | Operations Agent | Team input |
| Significant spend (>$[X]) | @ARK | Finance Agent |
| Hiring or firing | @ARK | Legal review |
| Legal commitment | @ARK | Legal Agent (`106`) |
| Strategic direction | @ARK | Full leadership |

### Escalation Path
1. Team member attempts resolution
2. Operations Agent reviews
3. @ARK final decision on unresolved issues

## 6. Project Management Framework

**Tool:** [INSERT: e.g., Linear, Notion, Asana]

**Structure:**
- Projects are broken into milestones with clear owners and due dates
- Every task has: title, owner, due date, and status
- Weekly standup reviews open blockers
- Monthly review assesses milestone progress

**Priority Levels:**
- P0: Drop everything — blocks revenue or causes legal/security risk
- P1: This week — significant operational impact
- P2: This sprint/month — important but not urgent
- P3: Backlog — good to have, no deadline
