# Investor FAQ — PulseOS

## Purpose
Provides high-signal answers for selective investor conversations during stealth. These answers are optimized for PulseOS's current stage: founder-led alpha development, cofounder formation first, and only limited anchor-angel participation before a later formal round.

## Product & Category

**Q: What is PulseOS broadly building?**  
**A:** PulseOS is building the company memory and company reality layer for the agentic workforce. We make companies machine-readable, grounded, and actionable for the agentic workforce. The category thesis is that model capability is advancing faster than company context infrastructure, and that serious agent deployment depends on two things at once: durable company memory about what has happened and a live company reality graph that reflects what is true right now.

**Q: Why keep the product details light?**  
**A:** Because the company is still in stealth and the alpha is in active development. At this stage we want to share enough to create conviction without over-exposing the implementation before the founding team is fully assembled.

**Q: Is this just another agent wrapper or workflow tool?**  
**A:** No. The thesis is infrastructure underneath agent workflows, not another point solution on top of existing models.

**Q: Why is this not simply a feature inside ChatGPT Enterprise or Claude Enterprise?**  
**A:** Because enterprise chat and enterprise runtime are different categories. Chat surfaces help users interact with models. PulseOS is focused on the runtime layer that decides what structured memory, live reality, evidence, permissions, tools, and skills an agent should get for a production workflow.

**Q: Why is the context layer economically important?**  
**A:** Because context control becomes compute control. The company that structures the context going into enterprise agent workflows can optimize the inference flowing through them, reduce customer cost, and ultimately become the inference layer for those workflows.

**Q: Why does neutrality matter in this category?**  
**A:** Because the more strategic the orchestration layer becomes, the less enterprises want their operating substrate tied to a single model vendor. A neutral runtime can sit across models, tools, policies, and workflows as enterprise needs evolve.

## Process & Timing

**Q: Why talk to investors before formally raising?**  
**A:** Because the highest-value investors at this stage can do more than provide capital. They can help recruit world-class cofounders, sharpen the narrative, and become informed early believers before a formal process begins.

**Q: Why stay in stealth right now?**  
**A:** Stealth protects focus. The alpha is being built, the founding team is still being assembled, and the company does not benefit from broad exposure before those pieces are stronger.

**Q: Why recruit cofounders before the formal round?**  
**A:** Because a company like PulseOS will be meaningfully shaped by the first three senior builders around the founder. The quality of that team affects architecture, investor credibility, and the strength of the formal pre-seed story.

**Q: Are you formally fundraising right now?**  
**A:** Not broadly. We are selectively speaking with investors and operators while building, and we may include a small number of anchor angels before the formal round if they are unusually additive.

**Q: What materials do you share now versus later?**  
**A:** Early conversations use teaser-safe materials only: the stealth one-pager, teaser deck, and founder formation memo. The full fundraising deck is reserved for post-team / pre-formal-round conversations once more disclosure is strategically appropriate.

## Team

**Q: What are the key roles you are recruiting?**  
**A:** Three founder-caliber roles: CTO, Chief AI Architect, and Enterprise Sales Lead.

**Q: What backgrounds are ideal for those roles?**  
**A:** We are targeting exceptional builders and GTM leaders with relevant density from places like Palantir, OpenAI, Anthropic, Databricks, Snowflake, Stripe, Retool, Perplexity, Salesforce Enterprise, ServiceNow, and comparable enterprise AI companies.

**Q: What is the most useful help right now?**  
**A:** High-quality introductions to founder-caliber talent for those three roles. That is the most leveraged contribution at this stage.

## Capital

**Q: Is there room for investors to get involved early?**  
**A:** Potentially, yes, but only in a limited way. There may be room for a small number of anchor angels ahead of the formal round. The bar is strategic value, not just capital.

**Q: What makes an investor useful right now?**  
**A:** Access to exceptional talent, deep understanding of infrastructure businesses, category credibility, and long-term alignment. The right investor helps build the company before they help price the round.

**Q: Why not just open the full pre-seed now?**  
**A:** Because it is better to open the formal process with a stronger team, a stronger alpha, and pre-warmed investor conviction. Doing it too early optimizes for speed instead of leverage.

**Q: Why will OpenAI or Anthropic not just build this?**  
**A:** They may build adjacent pieces, and that validates the market. Their natural center of gravity is the model platform, generic agent tooling, and enterprise access rails. PulseOS is focused on the neutral company runtime layer that decides what an agent should know, what it is allowed to access, and which tools and skills it should load for a specific workflow.

## Founder Context

**Q: Why is the founder building this company?**  
**A:** The insight comes from direct exposure to the context problem in earlier AI product work and later advisory work with AI-first companies. The same constraint kept appearing: capable models, weak company context, and too much bespoke integration for every real workflow.
