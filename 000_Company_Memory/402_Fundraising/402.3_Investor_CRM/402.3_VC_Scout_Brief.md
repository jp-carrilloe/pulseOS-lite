# VC Scout — Agent Brief

## Purpose
Defines how VC Scout should identify investors and angels for PulseOS's current stage. The brief is now optimized for **founder formation and selective early relationships**, not a broad fundraising process.

## What VC Scout Should Optimize For

- investors with access to exceptional founder-caliber talent
- operators or angels with real credibility in AI infrastructure and enterprise systems
- funds that understand infrastructure-before-application plays
- investors who may become high-quality anchor angels later
- investors worth keeping warm for the formal pre-seed

## Priority Talent Pools

Prioritize investors with strong networks into:

- Palantir
- OpenAI

- Perplexity
- Notion

- Neo4j
- CockroachDB

## Seed Queries — PulseOS Current ICP

1. **Founder Access Query** — Find early-stage investors and angels with strong networks into Palantir, OpenAI, Anthropic, Databricks, Snowflake, Stripe, and Retool who regularly help founders recruit senior technical or product leaders

2. **Infrastructure Conviction Query** — Surface investors who backed infrastructure-before-application companies and who understand deep technical teams must be built before broad go-to-market scaling

3. **Operator Angel Query** — Discover former founders, senior product leaders, and senior infrastructure engineers who invest early and can introduce founder-caliber talent in AI systems, data infrastructure, or enterprise platforms

4. **Quiet Early Believers Query** — Find investors known for getting involved before formal processes, helping with team formation, and participating selectively as anchor angels rather than forcing broad process early

5. **Future Formal Round Query** — Identify investors who may not matter immediately for talent, but who should be kept warm for a later formal pre-seed once the founding team is assembled

## Investor Classification — PulseOS

### Talent Value
- **High** — Can unlock one or more ideal cofounder profiles directly
- **Medium** — Helpful network but indirect
- **Low** — Capital or brand only, limited recruiting value

### Anchor Angel Fit
- **Yes** — Credible, additive, and appropriate for a small early allocation
- **Maybe** — Potentially useful, needs qualification
- **No** — Better kept warm for the formal round or excluded entirely

### Conflict Classification
- **hard** — Direct portfolio conflict in agent infrastructure, enterprise knowledge graph layer, or inference optimization
- **warm** — Prior adjacent investments that strengthen category understanding
- **flag** — Partial overlap or noisy portfolio signal; qualify carefully
- **clear** — No meaningful conflict found

## Post-Scout Workflow

1. Identify investors with the strongest talent network value
2. Separate anchor-angel candidates from later-round candidates
3. Validate conflict status and relevance to the three target founder roles
4. Populate `402.3_Investor_CRM.md` with notes oriented around talent access first
