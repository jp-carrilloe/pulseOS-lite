# Fundraising Strategy & Founder Formation Narrative — Tintto

## Purpose
Defines Tintto's current investor strategy during stealth. The immediate objective is not a broad fundraise. It is to assemble an exceptional founding team, sharpen conviction with a small number of high-value investors, and preserve the option for a selective anchor-angel allocation before a formal pre-seed process begins.

The fundraising narrative is staged across two deck types: teaser-safe materials for selective early conversations and a full fundraising deck for post-team / pre-formal-round use.

## Executive Summary

- Tintto is currently in stealth and in alpha development
- The founder has been building the product over the past couple of months
- Investor conversations are relationship-led and selective, not a broad fundraising process
- The primary ask is introductions to three world-class cofounders
- A small number of anchor angels may be added before the formal round if they are unusually strategic
- A broader pre-seed begins only after the founding team is assembled and the alpha story is stronger
- Fundraising materials are deliberately split between teaser assets now and a full fundraising deck later
- The strategic narrative is now anchored in a three-layer architecture: company memory, secure runtime and access management, and inference optimization

## Current Positioning (2026-04-09)

- **Stage:** Solo founder, working MVP, first paying client (€5K–€7K implementation)
- **Round 1 Status:** Open now — $300K–$500K from angels and accelerators; raise basis is current state
- **Round 2 Status:** Opens when 5 clients + 2 cofounders + OpenClaw OS prototype are live (target: 2026-06-18)
- **Accelerator Track:** YC Spring 2026 and EWOR applications active — acceptance replaces Round 1 angel close
- **Primary Objective:** Close Round 1 to fund the 10-week sprint; Round 2 opens on proof, not promise
- **Formal Raise Status:** Round 1 selectively open; not a broad process

## The Strategic Thesis

Tintto is building PulseOS as company memory for the agentic workforce. It makes companies machine-readable, grounded, and actionable for the agentic workforce. The category thesis remains large and venture-scale, but the company is intentionally keeping external disclosure tight while the alpha is built and the right founding team is assembled.

The architecture compounds in three layers:

- **Company memory** as the entry point and wedge
- **Secure runtime and access management** as the governed control layer above company memory
- **Inference optimization** as the long-run economic layer and eventual largest revenue driver

The strategic insight behind the current process is simple: for a company like Tintto, the first major leverage point is not maximizing investor volume. It is getting the founding team right. The architecture, product depth, and enterprise credibility of this company will be materially shaped by the first three senior builders who join.

That is why investor conversations happen now, before a broad formal raise:

- to validate resonance with high-context investors
- to unlock introductions to exceptional founder-caliber operators
- to identify a very small number of anchor angels who add outsized value
- to create informed demand before the broader pre-seed begins

## The Founding Team Being Assembled

### Founder / CEO
- Defines the category, company vision, ontology concept, and early company-building motion
- Leads narrative, founder recruiting, and early strategic investor relationships

### Chief AI Architect
- Owns reasoning systems, retrieval architecture, agent memory, tool use, and model orchestration
- Ideal backgrounds: OpenAI, Anthropic, Perplexity, Adept, LangChain

### Chief Technology Officer (CTO)
- Owns knowledge graph architecture, temporal modeling, source-system ingestion, enterprise data correctness, secure runtime, access control, governance infrastructure, reliability, and scalable systems
- Ideal backgrounds: Palantir, Databricks, Snowflake, Neo4j, CockroachDB, Stripe, Retool

### Enterprise Sales Lead
- Owns complex enterprise sales, AI/infrastructure GTM, consultative selling, design partners, security-review navigation, and conversion from executive strategy conversations to platform deployments
- Ideal backgrounds: Palantir, Salesforce Enterprise, Databricks, Snowflake, Stripe, Retool, ServiceNow, enterprise AI companies

## Ideal Investor Profile Right Now

### Prioritize
- Investors and angels with access to elite technical and product talent
- Investors who understand infrastructure-before-application plays
- Funds and operators with real networks into Palantir, OpenAI, Anthropic, Databricks, Snowflake, Stripe, Retool, Perplexity, Salesforce Enterprise, and ServiceNow
- People who can be useful before the formal round through introductions, strategic framing, and founder recruiting

### Secondary Priority
- Potential anchor angels who are:
  - highly credible with future pre-seed investors
  - willing to engage early without forcing a broad process
  - additive beyond capital

### Avoid
- Investors who push for a broad process before the team is assembled
- Low-context capital with little talent or category value-add
- Funds with direct portfolio conflicts in agent infrastructure, enterprise knowledge layers, or inference optimization

## Messaging Rules

- Do not present Tintto as broadly fundraising
- Do present Tintto as actively building
- Do signal that investor interest already exists without overstating process
- Do make the current ask founder-centric first and capital-centric second
- Do keep the tone selective, confident, and understated
- Do not share unnecessary product detail while in stealth
- Do use teaser materials in early-stage conversations and reserve the full fundraising deck for post-team / pre-formal-round disclosure

## Process Phases

### Phase 0: Round 1 — Angels & Accelerators (NOW)
- **Basis:** Solo founder + working MVP + first paying client
- **Ask:** $300K–$500K on a SAFE (uncapped MFN or $4M–$6M cap)
- **Audience:** Sigma Squared network angels, operator angels, YC/EWOR accelerators
- **Primary outcome:** Capital to fund the 10-week sprint; cofounder introductions; design partner introductions
- **Accelerator parallel track:** YC Spring 2026 and EWOR active — acceptance replaces the angel close
- **Target close:** 2026-05-15
- Use the One Pager and Teaser Deck; reserve Full Pitch Deck for post-Round 1 conversations

### Phase 1: 10-Week Sprint (2026-04-09 to 2026-06-18)
- Run 5 client implementations at €5K–€7K each using consultation-led model
- Build and launch OpenClaw OS prototype as MCP server
- Run weekly investor calls as value exchanges (not asks); use Sigma Squared fellow database as intel
- Identify and close 2 cofounder candidates
- Run daily content (LinkedIn/X) and podcast to warm prospects and build recruiting signal
- See [Strategy Roadmap](../402.0_Fundraising_Documents/402.0.0_Strategy_Roadmap.md) for full sprint detail

### Phase 2: Round 2 — Institutional Pre-Seed (opens 2026-06-18)
- **Basis:** 5 clients + 2 cofounders + OpenClaw OS live
- **Ask:** $1M–$2M on a SAFE at $8M–$12M post-money cap
- **Audience:** Infrastructure-focused pre-seed funds; strategic AI angels; accelerator follow-on
- **Primary outcome:** 18-month runway to $50K MRR and platform general availability
- Shift from teaser materials to full fundraising deck at this stage
- Target close: 2026-08-31
