## 2. Liability Framework for Agent Failures

As AI agents take autonomous or semi-autonomous actions, the following liability rules apply:

### What [CLIENT_NAME] is Liable For
- Agent outputs that are provably false and cause quantifiable client harm, where [CLIENT_NAME] had control over the agent's instructions
- Failure to deliver contractually defined services due to internal infrastructure failures
- Data breaches caused by [CLIENT_NAME]'s negligent handling of client data

### What [CLIENT_NAME] is NOT Liable For
- Third-party service failures (API outages, platform policy changes, data enrichment errors) — excluded in MSA Section 7.3
- Results not achieved due to client failing to fulfil obligations defined in SOW Section 4
- Indirect, consequential, or punitive damages — excluded in MSA Section 7.4
- Compliance with laws in the client's jurisdiction — client's responsibility per MSA Section 5.2

### Liability Cap
- Total liability is capped at fees paid by the client in the **[NUMBER] months** preceding the event
- Cap is defined in MSA Section 7.2 — ensure this is consistent across all active agreements

### Escalation Triggers
The following events trigger immediate escalation to @ARK and external legal counsel:

| Event | Trigger | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Data breach | Confirmed unauthorised access to personal data | Notify Legal, activate breach response, notify regulator within 72 hrs (GDPR) |
| Threatened legal action | Client or third party communicates legal threat | Preserve all records, do not respond without Legal Agent review |
| Agent causes reputational harm | Agent output causes public complaint or media attention | Suspend agent, assess root cause, notify client |
| Regulatory inquiry | Contact from data protection authority or regulator | Do not respond without legal review |

## 4. Dispute Resolution Protocol

**Step 1 — Good Faith Negotiation (0–30 days)**
Both Parties attempt to resolve the dispute through direct communication between senior representatives. No legal proceedings during this period unless immediate injunctive relief is required.

**Step 2 — Mediation (30–60 days)**
If negotiation fails, either Party may request non-binding mediation with a mutually agreed mediator. Costs split equally.

**Step 3 — Arbitration or Litigation**
If mediation fails, disputes proceed per the governing law and jurisdiction defined in the MSA.

**Record Preservation:**
From the moment a dispute is flagged — even informally — preserve all relevant communications, documents, and system logs. Do not delete or modify records related to the dispute.
